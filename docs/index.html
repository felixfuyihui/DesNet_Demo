DesNet: A multi-channel deep complexed neural network for joint speech dereverberation, enhancementand separation

Yihui Fu, Jian Wu, Yanxin Hu, Mengtao Xing, Lei Xie

Abstract:

 In this paper, we proposed an multi-channel deep complexed neural network for joint speech dereverberation, enhancement and separation (DesNet). To enable the gradient propagation and joint optimization of the those three modules, we adopted the attentional selection mechanism of the multi-channel features, which is originally proposed in end-to-end unmixing, fixed-beamforming and extraction (E2E-UFE) structure and the novel deep complex convolutional recurrent nework (DCCRN) as the structure of the speech unmixing. The neural network based WPE is cascaded beforehand for speech dereverberation. To further improve the final performance, we also introduced the staged SNR and discriminative loss strategy for speech enhancement and separation tasks. In our experiments, staged SNR improves 0.01 on PESQ in speech enhancement task and 1.22 dB on SI-SNR in speech separation task, while discriminative loss improves 0.13 on PESQ in speech enhancement task and 0.56 dB on SI-SNR in speech separation task.
